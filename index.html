About.

This planner's concept was originally designed by Jaco Drost to aid vegetable gardening, because the hardest, and most important part of gardening is planning. If you don't plan you will most likely plant too many of the same vegetable, or too little. Maybe you might plant all of your beds full at the same time and wait until finally your veggies are ready to be harvested. Just to find out you have too much vegetables. So you harvest too much veg for about two to three months, until all of your veggies die out. Then you have to do it all over again, while slowly your soil quality declines and your next harvest gets diseases and pests. Most people give up at this stage especially if you try organic gardening.

So you might be wondering how does planning resolve these problems? Well, the planner's purpose is to spread out sowing and planting trough out the year, in other words, planting a few things every month to have a continual harvest whole year round.

How.

The basic idea is to fill all of the months and beds with a large variety of vegetables with out adding the same family in the same bed twice, the families are displayed by their color, example : peas and beans should not both be in the same bed. Nor should you plant the same vegetable twice in the same bed.

To add a vegetable to the table simply click on the vegetable you want, then click it in the month you want to plant it, now the table shows how long the vegetable will be in the bed. The blue outline shows when you will likely harvest from this vegetable, now right next to the end of the vegetables growing period (when the veg will be taken out of the bed) plant your next vegetable, and continue until your bed is completely filled. To delete right click the begining of the vegetable you want to remove. Unfortunately there is no saving function therefore take a screenshot/print if you are happy with your plan.
